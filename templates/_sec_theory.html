theory — ABCD as a special case of MaxEnt

The hypothesis that frames every panel below: ABCD is MaxEnt with marginal constraints only. Keep the measured NN1–NN2 dependence and you generalize it.
ABCD METHOD
Two discriminants cut the plane into four regions; A is the signal region. If they are independent for background, the counts factorize and A is predicted from the three control regions.
Nₐ = Nᵝ · Nᶜ / Nᶥ
rests entirely on NN1 ⊥ NN2 in background.
MAXENT PRINCIPLE
Among all distributions consistent with the measured constraints, take the one of maximum entropy — the least-committal fit (Jaynes 1957; uniqueness, Shore–Johnson 1980). Marginals only ⇒ p(x)p(y).
max H(p) s.t. ⟨constraints⟩
add a correlation constraint ⇒ coupled joint.

▸ MAIN HYPOTHESIS
MaxEnt is a valid background-estimation method that generalizes ABCD: it predicts Nₐ from the control regions under marginal constraints plus the measured NN1–NN2 dependence — equal to ABCD when the scores are independent, and more accurate when they are not.
test — observed Nₐ vs both predictions across regions × cut modes; MaxEnt should track observed at least as well, strictly better where dependence is nonzero.
