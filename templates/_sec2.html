▸ the ABCD prediction error splits into two orthogonal requirements

The paper's §II shows the ABCD bias is the product of two independent failure modes. Reconstructing them on our data is the foundation of the method comparison — because only the first is something MaxEnt can touch.

Req 1 is a statement about background on the plane — the closure/independence axis, the only axis the MaxEnt copula corrects. Req 2 is a statement about signal leaking into the controls — it is method-invariant (same plane, same signal ⇒ identical r for ABCD and MaxEnt). Keeping them separate is what makes the comparison honest.

Two complementary independence reads. ORbg is the local, exact condition at this cut (= 1 means ABCD closes here). Debiased I(NN1;NN2) is the threshold-independent necessary condition: I=0 ⇔ ABCD closes at every cut. They are complementary, not redundant — a globally dependent plane (I>0) can still have ORbg≈1 at a particular cut.

requirement 2 — normalized signal contamination Eq 2.7–2.9

It is not enough that the signal fraction in each control is small (δi≪1, Eq 2.7). It must be small relative to the signal fraction in A. The paper's normalized contamination:

r = δA−1(δB + δC − δD),   δi = Ni,sNi,b,   require |r| ≪ 1

Per HSS mass point, mirroring the paper's Table II (δi per region, then r). δD enters negative (diagonally opposite, doubly suppressed) so r>0 ⇒ background over-prediction.

massδAδBδCδDr|r|≪1 ?

r is method-invariant. MaxEnt swaps the predictor on a fixed plane; it cannot change any δi. So this entire requirement is shared identically by ABCD and MaxEnt — which is exactly why MaxEnt cannot be placed on ABCDisCo's rejection-vs-r plane.

what the contamination costs — signal absorbed by ABCD's own prediction Eq 2.10

Expanding the prediction with signal present in all four regions:

NApred = NA,b(1 + δB + δC − δD) = NA,b(1 + δAr)

The contamination folds straight into the background estimate, so the signal excess that survives ABCD's own prediction is ∝ (1 − r). The bar shows the fraction of each signal's A-region excess that ABCD absorbs as background:

r>1 (light scalars here) means total absorption: ABCD predicts at or above the observed A-count even with signal present — no discoverable excess remains. Published illustration (ABCDisCo Fig 2): at δA=10%, NA=1000, the true p-value 0.0015 inflates to 0.03 / 0.10 with only 4% / 6% contamination in B — potential discoveries masked.

▸ one object underneath both: the 2×2 odds ratio

Requirement 1 is the odds ratio on background (ORbg — closure). Requirement 2's r is, via the paper's Eq 2.12, nearly the diagnostic odds ratio on signal. Both ABCD failure modes are the same 2×2 statement evaluated on the two sample types — which is why our information-theory stats layer (cell odds ratios, debiased MI, PMI) is the native language for this section, not a re-skin.

REQ 1: OR on background → closure  |  REQ 2: odds structure on signal → r

MaxEnt acts on REQ 1 only. The full method comparison (next) therefore compares ABCD vs MaxEnt on the closure axis, with r reported alongside as a shared property of the cut.
